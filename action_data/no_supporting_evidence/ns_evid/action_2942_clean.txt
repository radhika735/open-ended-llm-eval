2942: Provide alternative bat roosts during maintenance work at road/railway bridges and culverts
Effectiveness rating: Unknown effectiveness
Background information and definitions:
Bats may be temporarily excluded from their roosts in bridges or culverts during maintenance work to prevent injury or death. Alternative structures, such as bat houses, may be provided as temporary roosts until the work is complete.
For the effects of excluding bats, see ‘Exclude bats from roosts during maintenance work at road/railway bridges and culverts’.

Key Messages:

One study evaluated the effects of providing alternative bat roosts during maintenance work at road bridges. The study was in the USA.

COMMUNITY RESPONSE (0 STUDIES)
POPULATION RESPONSE (0 STUDIES)
BEHAVIOUR (1 STUDY)         

Use (1 study): One review in the USA found that bat houses provided as alternative roosts during bridge replacement works were used by fewer Mexican free-tailed bats than the original roost at one site and were not used by bats at all at three sites.
