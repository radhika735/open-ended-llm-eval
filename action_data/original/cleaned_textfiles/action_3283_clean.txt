3283: Actively manage water level before/after planting trees/shrubs: freshwater wetlands
Effectiveness rating: No evidence found (no assessment)
Background information and definitions:
This action involves active management of water levels in areas planted with marsh or swamp vegetation. It involves repeated management of the amount of water present and/or when it is present. This action will usually involve some kind of water control structure: a valve, gate, sluice or pump.
Related actions: Actively manage water level, other than to complement planting; one-off interventions to Raise water level before/after planting; Facilitate tidal exchange before/after planting; Irrigate before/after planting.

Key Messages:
We found no studies that evaluated the effects, on vegetation, of actively managing water levels in freshwater wetlands planted with trees/shrubs.
 
‘We found no studies’ means that we have not yet found any studies that have directly evaluated this action during our systematic journal and report searches. Therefore we have been unable to assess whether or not the action is effective or has any harmful impacts. Please get in touch if you know of such a study for this action.
