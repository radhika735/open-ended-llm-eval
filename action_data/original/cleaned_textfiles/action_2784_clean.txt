2784: Introduce alternative food sources to replace marine and freshwater mammal meat
Effectiveness rating: No evidence found (no assessment)
Background information and definitions:
The consumption of marine and freshwater mammal meat may provide a source of protein, particularly in areas of poverty and food insecurity (Robards & Reeves 2011). Introducing other food sources to replace marine and freshwater mammal meat may reduce hunting pressure.
Robards M.D. & Reeves R.R. (2011) The global extent and character of marine mammal consumption by humans: 1970–2009. Biological Conservation, 144, 2770–2786.

Key Messages:

We found no studies that evaluated the effects of introducing alternative food sources to replace marine and freshwater mammal meat.

‘We found no studies’ means that we have not yet found any studies that have directly evaluated this action during our systematic journal and report searches. Therefore we have been unable to assess whether or not the action is effective or has any harmful impacts. Please get in touch if you know of such a study for this action.
